🪨 Optional add-on: Castle Crags OPTIONAL

Right off I-5 between Mt. Shasta City and Redding (exit 724). Dramatic granite spires that rise 6,000 ft straight up — and the Vista Point gives you a frame-perfect view with Mt. Shasta in the background. Adds ~45 min to your drive home.

  • Vista Point (drive-up + 0.5 mi RT walk) — easy paved trail to a panoramic overlook. Zero effort, huge payoff.
  • Indian Creek Loop (1 mi, easy) — adds ~30 min if you want to stretch your legs
  • Crags Trail to Castle Dome (5.5 mi, 2,200 ft gain, strenuous) — only if you skipped Cinder Cone (you didn't)

Cost: $10 day-use fee at the state park entrance.

Recommendation: Stop only if you left Mt. Shasta City by 11:30 AM. Skip if running late — it's a great place but Mt. Shasta + Bunny Flat already gave you the headline view.

Packing checklist

  • Hiking boots (gaiters help with Cinder Cone's loose cinders)
  • 3 L water per person (no shade on Cinder Cone, Fire Watch = dry air)
  • Layers: fleece + windbreaker (40°F + 25 mph wind on the rim)
  • Sun hat, sunscreen, lip balm (intense sun at altitude)
  • Headlamp (Subway Cave + emergency)
  • Trekking poles (recommended for Cinder Cone descent)
  • Snacks + sandwiches for Sunday lunch (no services at Butte Lake)
  • Cash or card for park entry ($30 Lassen, $10 Burney)
  • Offline maps downloaded (no cell at Butte Lake) — Google Maps offline + AllTrails
  • Full tank of gas leaving Redding both days
